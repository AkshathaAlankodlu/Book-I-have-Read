The Alchemist

Author: Paulo Coelho

Genre: Fiction

Rating: 2.9

Review:The book keeps repeating the phrase "Soul of the World",which got me irritated. Even then i rated it a 2.9 because it wanted to convey the readers : 1)there is some purpose for everything that happens in our life 2)never regret you actions if it has helped you gain new experiences 3)do not go searching for something which is within you. I wouldnt recommend it to anyone. I regret reading it as a first book in 2025.Now that you know what is has you can completely skip it.

People We Meet on Vacation

Author: Emily Henry

Genre: Fiction,Romance

Rating: 4

Blurb: The story is about two friends Poppy and Alex who met 12 years ago and have promised to go on summer vacations every year since,wherever they end up in life. The book takes us through their journey every summer until after an incident on one of their trips. They end up just wishing each other on their birthdays for the next two years. It is finally Poppy who breaks the ice and offers to get back together for a trip after two long years of silence.

Review: Though it was a good read,it took me more than 3 weeks to finish the book. The main thing I liked about it was the bond Poppy and Alex shared. They were always there for eachother ,fun or pain. Distance was just a number for them. Even if Poppy thought she would never talk to Alex after their first meet, Alex becomes the main source of happiness in Poppy's life.

The Stillwater Girls

Author: Minka Kent

Genre: Fiction,Thriller

Rating: 4.5

Blurb: Wren and Sage are alone in there cabin hoping for the return of their Mama and younger sister Evie. Evie was ill when Mama left with her to find help for the illness 72 days ago.

Nicholitte on the other hand has been doubting her husband Brant of cheating her since the day she found a picture of a girl with the same green eyes as that of him in his sock drawer.Nic is not convinced until she finds the truth ,so she cancels her winter meet up with her friend Cate at New York only to find two girls at her doorstep asking for help.

Review: It was a quick read for me. I could finish it within a week. It even got my eyes filled towards the end when everything came to a happy ending. For a book with just 200 pages it had every scene explained such a way I could easily picture them in my mind. A fast paced reader would finish it in one go. You can definitely try this book if you are a beginner.

The Last One at the Wedding

Author: Jason Rekulak

Genre: Fiction,Thriller

Rating: 4.2

Blurb: It had been 3 years since Margeret disappeared from home. One evening, Frank receives a call from his daughter Margeret inviting him for her wedding with Aidan whome she met 6 months ago. Everything seems fine until Frank receives an image post with Aidan and an unfamiliar face next to him. It is after he finds that the girl in the photo is missing he starts getting suspicious of everything he sees and hears at the wedding venue.

Review: Initially I thought it was Frank's daughter who had been kidnapped but as the story went by I was curious to know what was happening and all the reasons behind it.Though at times I didnt like how the story went by,I realised that the author was trying to show how a child who grows without her mother's care ends up in life.Busy in earning for their living Frank could never pay much attention to his daughter,at times when he actually spent time and things went wrong he was unable to handle it himself which made the situation hard for the two eventually breaking that father-daughter bond. I truly appreciate Frank's decision at the end and was really happy that Frank had Abigal to show all his fatherly love.
